Solar Energy



SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY


With our products, our way of working and our company decisions, we make a contribution to the development of a sustainable, fair and humane form of society and economy with a rapid switch to a solar/regenerative energy supply.

We consciously take responsibility for the entire development of our products. When purchasing our components, we therefore strive for short supply routes, among other things. For imported items, we try to strictly avoid supporting inhuman work structures.


Solar Projects

Heres a list of our available and completed solar energy projects

Karoo Fresh - Solar & Storage Crowdsale Phase 2 | 332.10 kW Solar + 640 kWh Storage | Karoo


This is Phase 2 of Karoo Fresh’s solar and energy storage crowdsale. In another first for Stockminers members, this 2nd phase crowdsale opens while construction is already underway, giving you limited opportunity to purchase solar cells already waiting on-site to be installed, with completion scheduled for the end of August 2022.

Tamboerskloof Primary School | 32.70 kW Solar + 14.3 kWh Storage | Cape Town


This will be Stockminers's first school solar plant equipped with a battery storage system, meaning that they can keep on teaching even during the routine power-cuts that are impacting education all over the country. By purchasing solar cells for this project, you will positively impact education in South Africa and earn with purpose.

La Recolte Retirement Village | 57.24 kW Solar + 14.3 kWh Storage | Cape Town


La Recolte Retirement Village is situated in the beautiful area of Stellenberg in the Western Cape. Its suburban living offers residents the comfort of remaining active while enjoying the vibrant lifestyle of the Northern Suburbs in Cape Town. By purchasing solar cells you’re bringing savings to the elderly.

La Avontuur Estate | 122.58 kW Solar + 14.3 kWh Storage | Stellenbosch, South Africa


Avontuur Estate is a family business situated against the Helderberg Mountains near South Africa’s famed Stellenbosch wine region. Avontuur deeply depends on climate and nature across all aspects of its business. The estate is going solar as part of its desire to be more sustainable and environmentally-responsible.
